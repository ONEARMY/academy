## üîç Search

The first step of setting up a workspace is to find a space (duh). While searching, there are a few considerations to keep in mind.

1. <b>Size:</b> It‚Äôs important to be sure that you have enough space to move around freely with your machines and tools. The injection space we designed is roughly 4 x 6 meters ‚Äì you can go larger, but if you include the shredder (like ours) we wouldn‚Äôt recommend going much smaller.

2. <b>Electricity:</b> The Injection machine can run on single-phase, but the shredder sometimes runs on three-phase, so be sure to check the specs of the motor you plan to use when choosing your space.

3. <b>Budget:</b> While making sure your space meets your functional needs, it‚Äôs also important to keep in mind how it will fit into your budget. 

> Pro-tip: sometimes, cities offer discounted rent to entrepreneurs with purposeful missions, so it‚Äôs worth getting in touch with accelerators and representatives of your municipality to see if there are opportunities like this in your area.

## üõ† Build

Now that your floor plan is blocked out, it‚Äôs time to transform your empty room into a Mix workspace.

### Begin with basics

Start with your most essential items - machines, workbench, basic tools ‚Äì then build out from there as you start to see what suits your workflow best.

When possible, look for secondhand options to reduce the amount of new materials and resources being used to start your space.

If what you need isn‚Äôt available secondhand, buy new or build.

Some essential items to get you started:

- Machines - of course
- Pre-shredded plastic storage containers - bags work well, might depend on how you collect your plastic
- Shredded plastic storage containers ‚Äì Can be almost anything. What‚Äôs most important is to make sure that they‚Äôre sturdy to prevent accidental spills. If you have your own shredder, it‚Äôs nice to use storage containers that you can shred into then store, so that you don‚Äôt have to pour shredded plastic from one container to another ‚Äì helps keep types and colors pure. Clear containers are nice, but not required.
- Simple msold ‚Äì could be beam, bowl, or any other item ‚Äì depends on which machines you use. Best to start with something simple to familiarize yourself with the process then build from there. Check out the how-to‚Äôs for inspiration
- Basic tools - wrenches, scoop, hammer, chisel, channel lock pliers, screwdriver, safety glasses, heat resistant gloves
- Ventilation ‚Äì filter mask, ventilation caddy, check out the safety video to learn more
- Workbench
- Offcut container


Once you‚Äôve got your basic workspace set up, start working with the extruder to develop your workflow and see what else might help you work more efficiently and comfortably.

And don‚Äôt be afraid to spice the place up with your own unique charm :) Plants, posters, the right lighting, and a boombox will make your workspace more enjoyable to be in for yourself and anyone who stops by.
